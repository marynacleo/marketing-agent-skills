# Feel-First Storyboards: blank template + worked examples

## Blank template (copy per video)

```
VIDEO: [working title]
BRAND: [brand]            PERSONA: [one persona]        JOURNEY STAGE: [discover/consider/decide/after-sale]
ONE MESSAGE: [a single sentence the viewer should feel, not read]
ONE EMOTION (scene 4): [relief / pride / calm / joy / belonging / ...]
PLATFORM: [Reels / TikTok / Shorts]   TARGET LENGTH: [12-20 s]
PRODUCTION PATH: [real footage / AI / hybrid]

SCENE 1 - HOOK (2 s)
  Image: [the single most sensory image]
  Framing/move: [extreme close-up / unexpected POV + slow push-in]
  Sound: [ambient detail]

SCENE 2 - SITUATION (4 s)
  Image: [persona in real life, tension or desire visible]
  Framing/move: [handheld chest height, family-member distance]
  Sound: [room tone + one diegetic detail]

SCENE 3 - TRANSFORMATION (4 s)
  Image: [the change happening on screen; product enters as enabler]
  Framing/move: [hands and faces, slow confident move]
  Sound: [sound of the work itself]

SCENE 4 - CLOSE + CTA (2-6 s)
  Image: [resting gesture, emotion lands; negative space for caption]
  Framing/move: [static or drifting, medium close-up]
  CTA text (edit only, no final period): [one soft line]
  Sound: [ambience settles / soft music resolves]

LIGHT (all scenes): [natural source, time of day]
CONTINUITY KIT: subject description = [...] / setting phrase = [...] / grading = [...]
CHECK: one persona? one stage? one message? product only in scene 3? both languages if bilingual?
```

## Worked example A: service brand (web studio)

```
VIDEO: The night before the launch
BRAND: a web studio       PERSONA: solo founder, 35-50, launching her first site
STAGE: consider           MESSAGE: "someone finally took this weight off my shoulders"
EMOTION: relief           PLATFORM: Reels, 16 s   PATH: AI (Sora/Kling)

S1 HOOK: laptop glow on a tired face in a dark kitchen, coffee gone cold, macro on the cup
S2 SITUATION: she scrolls her half-broken site at midnight, sighs, kids' drawings on the fridge
S3 TRANSFORMATION: morning; a video call; a calm hand shares a clean new homepage on screen;
   her shoulders visibly drop
S4 CLOSE: she closes the laptop, looks out the window, small exhale-smile
   CTA: "Your launch, handled"
LIGHT: S1-S2 warm lamp in dark, S3-S4 soft morning window light
```

## Worked example B: travel brand (bespoke tours)

```
VIDEO: The table you didn't book
BRAND: a bespoke travel brand   PERSONA: couple 30-45, tired of packaged tours
STAGE: discover           MESSAGE: "travel that feels found, not booked"
EMOTION: belonging        PLATFORM: TikTok, 18 s   PATH: AI or real footage from trips

S1 HOOK: steam rising off dumplings, hands reaching in, laughter off-screen
S2 SITUATION: the couple hesitates at a crowded tourist street, map in hand, noise
S3 TRANSFORMATION: a local host waves them through a gate into a courtyard table under vines;
   wine poured, POV sitting down
S4 CLOSE: her head on his shoulder at dusk, mountains behind, glasses half full
   CTA: "The real country, not the brochure"
LIGHT: golden hour throughout, courtyard shade in S3
```

## Worked example C: local trade brand (home improvement)

```
VIDEO: The bill that stopped hurting
BRAND: a local installer  PERSONA: homeowner 40-60, worried about rising bills
STAGE: decide             MESSAGE: "this house finally works for us, not against us"
EMOTION: quiet pride      PLATFORM: Reels, 15 s   PATH: hybrid (real site footage + AI b-roll)

S1 HOOK: morning sun crawling across a kitchen table, a folded utility bill in the light
S2 SITUATION: the homeowner at the table, calculator, small frown, garden outside the window
S3 TRANSFORMATION: installers' gloved hands at work on the house, bolts tightened, a panel
   levelled; the homeowner watches from the doorway with a coffee
S4 CLOSE: evening; family dinner in warm light; the bill lies forgotten under a fruit bowl
   CTA: "Ask for your free estimate"
LIGHT: S1-S2 hard morning sun, S3 bright midday, S4 warm interior evening
```

Use these as PATTERNS: the scene logic transfers to any sector. Never copy one
brand's palette, personas or tone onto another brand.
