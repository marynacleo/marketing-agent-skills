# Page composer (modular, not a fixed PAS template)

Do not force every page through Problem-Agitate-Solution. PAS is one available
pattern, best for cold, problem-unaware traffic. A visitor who already searched
for a specific tour with a date and price should not have to wade through pain
agitation to reach the button.

## Required core (every page answers these, in an order that fits the intent)

1. What is this? (headline that honors the ad promise)
2. Is it relevant to me? (message match, the specific segment)
3. What do I get? (offer, mechanism, concrete outcome)
4. Why should I trust it? (proof: verified, specific)
5. What does it cost or require? (price, terms, effort, objection handling)
6. What happens next? (one clear CTA, repeated with identical wording)

## Optional modules (select by intent and awareness stage)

pricing, dates, itinerary or specifications, comparison, process/how-it-works,
testimonials, case studies, guarantees, scarcity/availability, eligibility,
FAQ, personalization.

## Choose structure by awareness stage

- **Problem / solution-unaware (cold):** lead with the problem, use PAS, build
  the case before the offer.
- **Product-aware / most-aware (hot):** lead with the offer and proof in the
  hero, cut the warm-up, remove friction to the CTA.

## CRO invariants

- One primary action per page. Secondary links must not compete with it.
- Repeat the SAME CTA (wording, color, shape) in hero, after proof, and at close.
- Proof near every claim; place a trust block right after the first CTA.
- Concrete copy, real numbers, no vague benefit language.
- Semantic HTML, accessible forms, fast mobile render (see performance-gate.md).
- Headings without a final period; no em dashes in public copy.

For deeper copy or CRO work, hand off lazily to the project's copy/CRO skills
(see measurement-and-attribution.md and the SKILL.md adjacent-skills contract).
